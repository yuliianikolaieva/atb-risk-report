ATB Additional Agreement — Decision Memo

Confidential · For management approval · Bolt Operations Ukraine × LLC «ATB-MARKET» · June 2026

Full report (online): ATB Additional Agreement — Risk Assessment

1. Context

  • ATB is the #1 national retail chain in Ukraine — ~50% of the modern grocery market, ~1,300+ stores nationwide — and a major growth opportunity for Bolt Food.
  • Deal value (GMV forecast): ~€4M in 2026 (Sep–Dec) · ~€14.6M in 2027 (full year) · ~€18.6M cumulative through 2027 — weighed against the contractual risks below.
  • Negotiation has run for 1+ year on ATB's standard form of contract; each round of comments took 2 weeks to 1 month. The remaining step is to agree the tax addendum — assessed by the auditors, with the commercial risks assessed on our side.
  • ATB does not make exceptions. All partners operate under ATB's standard flow and documents; the additional agreement is effectively non-negotiable — everyone signs the same terms.

2. What the agreement is

An Additional Agreement to the services contract that shifts tax, criminal-procedure and registration risk onto Bolt (as Contractor), with broad one-sided liability and a set of penalties. 20 clauses were flagged by auditors (UHY + Baker McKenzie). Bolt's revenue from ATB ≈ 13.33% of GMV (3.33% commission + 10% Service Fee); the penalty base under the contract is only the 3.33% commission — the Service Fee is collected from consumers.

4. Scenarios

Scenario A — Worst case (all risks materialise)

≈ €700k / yr (2027)
extra low probability
  • ≈ 4.8% of GMV, ≈ 36% of Bolt's revenue (~€1.95M incl. SF).
  • Cumulative 2026–2027 ≈ €892k.
  • Legal risk is bounded: penalties (~€700k) stay well below revenue (~€1.95M) and are ~2× covered by the Service Fee — the legal risk does not exceed revenue.
  • Service Fee covers it — calculation: SF (10% of GMV) ≈ €1.46M (2027) − worst-case loss ~€700k = +€760k (SF ÷ loss ≈ 2.1×); the Service Fee fully absorbs the worst-case penalty.
  • Net P&L: ATB is Pre-HQ EBITDA negative standalone (≈ −€769k) — a strategic investment; with worst-case penalties ≈ −€1.47M.
  • Drivers: unlimited liability (Cl. 2 / 2.1), VAT (20%) + CIT (18%) compensation + budget recovery (100%), payment suspension, criminal-proceedings trigger.
  • Plus cash-flow (timing, not a loss): 20% of payment deferred until tax-invoice registration (Cl. 6, ~€97k) and payment suspension (Cl. 2.2/2.3, up to ~€486k) — recovered later, affects working capital only.

Scenario B — Base case (mitigated)

≈ €10–50k / yr
max — high probability to land on zero losses

Residual, realistically enforceable exposure. We accept and mitigate because:

  • 10-year track record — not a single such case has occurred.
  • Clear UHY (accounting firm supplier) algorithm — a defined, clause-by-clause working process for every point of this addendum and the contract, agreed with UHY, who share responsibility for these clauses.
  • Fast-reaction flow — at the first sign of any risk we block/suspend work until it is clarified.
  • Court practice — enforcement is effectively impossible; the underlying law is disputable (Supreme Court, 06.02.2026, No. 904/2953/24: contractual transfer of tax liability is invalid).
Recommendation — GM & local Retail team

We recommend signing the agreement in its current edition and moving forward. ATB's strategic value (national scale, ~50% market) clearly outweighs the residual legal risk, which is largely unenforceable and further contained by the mitigations above. We commit to delivering Scenario B — we will set up and run the mitigation process (clause-by-clause algorithm with UHY, fast-reaction blocking flow at the first sign of any risk) so that risks stay managed.
